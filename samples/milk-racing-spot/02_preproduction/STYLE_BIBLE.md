# Style Bible — Fuel the Fastest

Aesthetic throughline so the three shots read as one campaign.

## Palette

- **Primary warmth:** golden-hour amber (#e8b86a-ish), warm cream (#f2e6cf), sun-kissed skin tones
- **Accents:** charcoal racing graphite (#2d2d33), deep stadium shadow (#1a1817)
- **Hero color:** pure white milk — both the product and the visual motif (pour splash, spray arc)
- **Rule:** every frame carries warm amber highlights. No cool-blue-dominant frames. No neon/saturated accents.

## Light

- **Direction:** warm key from upper-camera-left at all three beats, suggesting late-afternoon sun
- **Quality:** soft-diffused in shot 02 (product); harder with stadium lens flare in shots 01 and 03
- **Accents:** slight anamorphic flare across the frame edge acceptable; no flashy JJ-Abrams lens flare bursts

## Lens / camera language

- Shot 01: wide anchor, cinema-camera look, shallow DoF, slight motion blur only where warranted (crew hands)
- Shot 02: macro beauty shot, extreme shallow DoF
- Shot 03: slightly low angle (hero), medium telephoto feel (85mm–135mm equivalent), clean composition

## Materiality

- Race car: modern formula-style, matte-charcoal with cream accents. No brand logos anywhere. No sponsor decals.
- Crew outfits: warm charcoal + cream livery, understated. No team crests.
- Milk bottle: simple matte-black sports bottle, condensation, stainless accents. No label, no logo.
- Trophy: classic silver motorsport cup, unadorned.

## Register (what this is NOT)

- Not cartoonish or playful — the humor is in the juxtaposition, not in the rendering
- Not kid-focused — this is a prestige commercial, adult audience
- Not retro Americana — this is modern international motorsport aesthetic
- Not muscle-car drag-racing — this is precision open-wheel / formula-style

## Consistency enforcers (what repeats across all 3 shots)

- Golden-hour warmth in the key light
- Matte-charcoal + cream livery (car, crew, bottle)
- Slight anamorphic lens flare
- Clean, logo-free surfaces
- Shallow depth of field
